# Playability Audit

## Verdict

Version 0.6 has enough decision points for a compact solo game, provided the
new Stewardship and Harvest rules remain central. Before that addition, the
game had several interesting military choices but regional recovery was mostly
automatic. Agriculture appeared as damage rather than policy.

The game does not currently need a general vassal system. Such a system would
be relevant mainly to English Mercia and later scenarios, while Harvest affects
every Muster in every period. Mercian dependence is better expressed through
scenario rules, the allied force, and Command cards until testing demonstrates
that named political relationships need more resolution.

## Meaningful Choices Per Round

A normal round asks the player to decide:

1. whether to replace a Command card;
2. whether to use one card for Command or Kingdom;
3. which two of the six different Basic Orders to take;
4. where to Muster and which homes should bear service;
5. whether to Steward land or spend tempo on the immediate war;
6. what purpose a confrontation should serve;
7. whether to release or retain fyrds at Service 3;
8. during Harvest, which fyrds remain in the field at the cost of home yield;
9. which safe region receives the one free recovery.

Not every round uses all nine decisions. A healthy round should contain four
to six consequential choices and no more than one procedural choice.

## Strong Decisions

- **Muster geography:** force strength and regional condition are the same
  decision.
- **Command versus Kingdom:** card opportunity cost connects war and rule.
- **Steward versus military tempo:** damaged land now consumes an order if the
  player wants rapid recovery.
- **Harvest release:** a field force has a visible economic cost at specific,
  predictable moments.
- **Battle purpose:** Delay, Drive Off, Force Decision, and Blockade pursue
  different operational outcomes.
- **Negotiation:** strength, legitimacy, camps, and terms interact without a
  separate diplomacy phase.

## Remaining Risks

- **Reform specialization:** S04, S05, and S08 can reward repeated Kingdom
  effects more than map play. Human tests should reject any opening that treats
  Reform as an isolated point engine.
- **Steward priority:** always restoring the lowest Readiness would make the
  choice mechanical. Raids, routes, Burh sites, and Harvest thresholds must
  regularly change the preferred region.
- **Household safety:** the permanent forces may make small confrontations too
  cheap. Track how often players fight without a fyrd commitment.
- **Delay loops:** repeated Delay could neutralize a Host without enough cost.
  Record consecutive uses against the same Host.
- **Scenario mandates:** mandates must alter play before the final round, not
  become an endgame checklist.

## Simulation Interpretation

The simulator compares broad martial, adaptive, and reform priorities. It does
not play every card, route, or textual Historical Mandate literally. Its
reported **core rate** covers only the structured objective. Version 0.6 is
useful for detecting impossible objectives and dominant strategic postures,
but not for proving full scenario win rates or balance.

Current intended bands on King difficulty:

- S01 learning: approximately 55-70%;
- S03-S05 standard: approximately 45-60%;
- S02 and S07 severe: approximately 25-45%;
- S06 and S08 ordeal: approximately 20-35% for adaptive play.

## Advancement Gate

Do not add vassal loyalty tracks unless at least two of these occur in human
testing:

- Mercian Aid is an automatic choice whenever available;
- S05 cannot distinguish alliance from ordinary Reform spending;
- Mercian withdrawal would create a meaningful loss state in S06 or S07;
- players ask who controls or owes service in a way the current rules cannot
  answer.

Until then, agriculture and service should carry the design. They are Alfred's
clearest mechanical identity and the strongest distinction from *IMPERATOR*.

## Excellence Gate

A public rating cannot be designed or guaranteed. Before release, require:

- blind third-play rules ratings with a median of at least 8.5/10;
- at least 70% of blind testers choosing an immediate replay;
- no repeated rules question from more than 15% of testers;
- perceived agency, decision clarity, and thematic integration each at 9/10
  median;
- no scenario outside its target win-rate band after at least 30 experienced
  plays; and
- no dominant opening above the 70% threshold in any scenario.

These gates are harder and more useful than claiming a future BoardGameGeek
score. Missing one is a development task, not a marketing exception.
