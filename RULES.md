# Rules of Play

## 1. Your Purpose

You are Alfred of Wessex. Survive the chosen Scenario, complete its Historical
Mandate, and leave a kingdom still able to answer another invasion.

You lose immediately if:

- Legitimacy or Obligation reaches 0;
- a Scenario's named royal seat is occupied at the end of a round; or
- you cannot remove a required West Saxon force.

At the end of the final round, you win if the Scenario objective and Historical
Mandate are complete. Your legacy score is:

`Legitimacy + Obligation + Wealth + Ready regions + active Burhs - fortified camps`

Battlefield success matters, but an exhausted kingdom cannot earn the highest
legacy.

## 2. The Map

The map has ten connected regions:

- **Exeter**, **Athelney**, **Wareham**, **Winchester**, **Wilton**, and
  **Reading** form the heart of Wessex.
- **Kent** guards the eastern coast.
- **London** controls the lower Thames.
- **Wallingford** is the main upper Thames approach into Wessex.
- **English Mercia** represents the allied Mercian polity and northern road.

Printed connections are roads, river routes, or coastal approaches. Some
connections are marked **River**. Danish Hosts may use them when their Intent
permits; West Saxon forces use all connections normally.

Each region has one fyrd piece and one Readiness state:

- **Ready**: its fyrd may be mustered normally.
- **Depleted**: its fyrd may be raised only by an Emergency Muster.
- **Exhausted**: its fyrd cannot be raised.

Readiness is the condition of the region, not the strength of a unit. A fyrd
piece in the field always contributes 1 Force.

Athelney, Winchester, Reading, Kent, London, and English Mercia can contain a
**Burh**. A Burh is either inactive or active. An active Burh provides:

- +1 West Saxon total when defending its region;
- protection from the first Readiness loss caused by a raid there; and
- a delay: a Danish Host entering the empty region must stop.

## 3. Forces and Armies

Alfred has:

- two permanent Household force pieces;
- one temporary Mercian allied force piece;
- one fyrd piece belonging to each region; and
- one Alfred marker.

Household forces do not affect regional Readiness and remain in play unless
lost. Fyrd pieces remember their home region.

There are four Danish Hosts. Each has a marker, Strength from 0-6, a doctrine,
and a visible Intent:

- **Guthrum's Host - Winter Seizure:** gains +1 in a region containing an
  inactive Burh or royal seat.
- **Seaborne Host - Coastal Mobility:** may move between connected coastal regions
  when Raiding, even if the printed connection is not their shortest route.
- **Hæsten's Host - Feigned Terms:** the first negotiation with it each
  Scenario costs 1 additional Wealth.
- **Thames Host - River Penetration:** may move two River connections when its
  Intent is Seek Camp.

The four Intents are:

- **Raid:** move toward the nearest Wessex region that is Ready; if none is
  Ready, target the nearest Depleted Wessex region. Raid if able.
- **Seek Camp:** move toward the nearest inactive Burh or royal seat; establish
  a fortified camp if the region is undefended.
- **Draw the Fyrd:** threaten the nearest Ready region not containing Alfred;
  if opposed, withdraw one connection after reducing that region's Readiness.
- **Join:** move toward the strongest other Danish Host. If they meet, both
  gain +1 Strength, then change this Host's Intent to Raid.

Scenario and Crisis cards tell you which Host acts and its Intent. One Host
normally performs one action. There is no opposition flowchart.

For equal targets or routes, choose the one closest to the Scenario's royal
seat, then alphabetically. If an Intent has no legal target, the Host Musters:
increase its Strength by 1 instead of moving.

## 4. Tracks

Tracks run from 0-7 unless stated otherwise:

- **Legitimacy:** confidence in Alfred's rule.
- **Obligation:** willingness and ability of regions to answer royal demands.
- **Wealth:** silver, food, gifts, transport, and administrative capacity.
- **Service:** how long the current field army has remained assembled, 0-3.
- **Reform:** durable defensive and political work, 0-6.

If Legitimacy or Obligation reaches 0, you lose. Wealth may reach 0.

## 5. Setup

1. Choose a Scenario and place Danish Hosts, Strength, Burhs, Readiness, and
   tracks as listed.
2. Place the two Household forces and Alfred in the Scenario's royal seat.
3. Put every available fyrd piece in its home region's reserve.
4. Shuffle the eight Royal Priority cards, reveal two, and choose one. Return
   the other to the box and keep the chosen card face up.
5. Shuffle the 16 Command cards and draw three.
6. Sort the Crisis cards into eight two-card groups, A-H. Keep the Scenario's
   printed group sequence visible.
7. Set Service to 0 and begin Round 1.

For a first game use **S01 The Year of Battles**.

Choose a challenge level:

- **Chronicle:** gain 1 Obligation after setup.
- **King:** use the printed setup.
- **Ordeal:** lose 1 Wealth and increase the strongest starting Host by 1.

Scenario difficulty assumes **King** level.

## 6. Round Sequence

### I. Receive Intelligence

Read the current round's group from the Scenario. Shuffle that group's two
cards, reveal one, and set the other aside. Apply its **Arrival** text, then
place its named Intent beside the active Danish Host. Return both cards to
their group after the round.

### II. Hold Council

You may discard one Command card and draw one replacement.

Choose one card from your hand and use exactly one half:

- **COMMAND:** its military or operational effect; or
- **KINGDOM:** its civil, diplomatic, or reform effect.

Discard it, then draw back to three cards.

### III. Issue Two Orders

Take two different Basic Orders:

- **Muster:** choose Alfred's region as the gathering region. Move the fyrd
  from that region and up to two adjacent Ready regions to Alfred's region.
  Each contributing region becomes Depleted. Set Service to at least 1.
- **March:** move Alfred and any number of West Saxon forces with him one
  connection. Alternatively move up to two Household forces one connection.
- **Confront:** resolve a battle, interception, or blockade where Alfred and a
  Danish Host share a region.
- **Fortify:** pay 2 Wealth and reduce a Ready region to Depleted to activate
  its Burh. Gain 1 Reform.
- **Negotiate:** if Alfred is in or adjacent to a Danish Host's region, pay 1
  Wealth and test terms as described in section 9.
- **Steward:** you may first return any number of fyrds with Alfred to their
  home reserves. If you return the last field fyrd, set Service to 0 and gain
  1 Obligation. Then recover one eligible region by one step. If it becomes
  Ready, gain 1 Wealth. A region is eligible if its fyrd is not in the field,
  no Host or fortified camp is there, and it was not raided or mustered this
  round.

An **Emergency Muster** may raise one Depleted region in or adjacent to
Alfred's region. Move its fyrd into the field, make the region Exhausted, and
lose 1 Obligation. It replaces the normal effect of Muster.

### IV. Danish Design

Apply the Crisis card's **Design** text. Then the active Host performs its
Intent:

1. If West Saxon forces share its region, resolve a Clash.
2. Otherwise move it one connection toward its target.
3. If it enters an undefended region, resolve its Intent there.

When a Danish Host raids an undefended region:

- reduce that region's Readiness by one step;
- lose 1 Wealth, or 1 Legitimacy if Wealth is 0;
- if the region has an active Burh, deactivate the Burh instead of reducing
  Readiness; and
- if the Host's Intent is Seek Camp, place a fortified camp there.

A fortified camp adds +1 to that Host in its region and prevents Readiness
recovery there. Remove the camp after Alfred wins a battle there or completes
two consecutive Blockades there.

### V. Service, Harvest, and Recovery

Some Scenario rounds are marked **Harvest**. Before increasing Service in a
Harvest round, resolve every fyrd still in the field:

- **Release it:** return it to its home reserve; or
- **Keep it:** reduce its home region's Readiness by one step.

Then count Ready Wessex regions:

- 5 or more: gain 2 Wealth;
- 3-4: gain 1 Wealth;
- 0-2: gain no Wealth.

Harvest uses the existing Readiness states. It adds no crop cubes or separate
agriculture track. A region cannot both sustain a field army and provide its
full seasonal yield.

If at least one fyrd began the round in the field and remains there, increase
Service by 1. Fyrds raised this round do not advance Service immediately. When
Service reaches 3, choose:

- **Release:** return every fyrd to its home reserve and set Service to 0; or
- **Retain:** keep the army, set Service to 1, make one contributing region
  Exhausted, and lose 1 Obligation.

Service never causes another automatic loss.

Then choose one region to recover one step, Exhausted to Depleted or Depleted
to Ready. The chosen region must meet all of these conditions:

- its fyrd is not in the field;
- no Danish Host or fortified camp is there;
- it was not raided, mustered, or used to build a Burh this round.

Check defeat, the Historical Mandate, and the Scenario objective. Advance the
round.

## 7. Battle

Before rolling, choose one purpose:

- **Delay:** +1 West Saxon total. On any result except a loss by 3+, prevent the
  Host's next movement. A win does not reduce Strength.
- **Drive Off:** use the standard result.
- **Force Decision:** requires at least three West Saxon forces. Add +2 West Saxon total.
  On a tie or loss, remove one additional fyrd after resolving the result.
- **Blockade:** requires an active Burh or fortified camp in the region. Do not
  roll. Pay 1 Wealth, prevent the Host from moving this round, and reduce its
  Strength by 1 if it was already blockaded last round.

For a battle, commit any number of West Saxon forces in Alfred's region.

`West Saxon total = committed Forces + West Saxon die + Burh + card bonuses`

`Danish total = Host Strength + Danish die + camp + doctrine bonuses`

Resolve the difference:

- **West Saxon win by 3+:** reduce Strength by 2, remove its camp, and retreat it two
  connections toward its entry region. Gain 1 Legitimacy.
- **West Saxon win by 1-2:** reduce Strength by 1 and retreat it one connection.
- **Tie:** both remain; either lose 1 Wealth or increase Service by 1.
- **West Saxon loss by 1-2:** remove one committed fyrd or lose 1 Legitimacy.
- **West Saxon loss by 3+:** remove one committed force, reduce its home region to
  Exhausted if it was a fyrd, and lose 1 Legitimacy.

A removed fyrd returns to its regional reserve. A removed Household force is
lost for the Scenario.

## 8. Clash

A Clash is a Danish-initiated battle:

1. Commit any West Saxon forces already in the region.
2. Roll both dice using normal totals.
3. On a West Saxon win, reduce Strength by 1 and retreat the Host.
4. On a tie, both remain and increase Service by 1 if a fyrd fought.
5. On a loss by 1-2, reduce one committed fyrd's region to Exhausted.
6. On a loss by 3+, remove one committed force and lose 1 Legitimacy.

A Clash cannot use battle purposes and grants no Legitimacy for winning.

## 9. Negotiation

To negotiate with a Host, roll one die and add:

- +1 if Alfred has at least as many forces nearby as the Host's Strength;
- +1 if Legitimacy is 5+;
- +1 if the Host is at Strength 2 or less.

On 6+, choose two. On 4-5, choose one. On 1-3, the Host gains +1 Strength.

- The Host retreats to its entry region.
- Remove one fortified camp.
- Gain 1 Legitimacy.
- Reduce the Host's Strength by 1.
- Mark an Oath: ignore its next activation, then remove the Oath.

After a successful negotiation, lose 1 Legitimacy if you paid Wealth while a
different Danish Host occupied a Wessex region. Terms can preserve the kingdom,
but they are visible political choices.

## 10. Royal Priority

Your Royal Priority describes the kind of king Alfred intends to become. It
adds no phase and is never required for victory.

- Its **Royal Resolve** may be used once per Scenario at the printed time.
  Rotate the card after using it.
- At Scenario end, add 2 to the legacy score if its **Legacy** condition is
  complete. The bonus cannot turn a loss into a win.

Choosing between two priorities gives each play a different tactical reserve
and long-term temptation without adding another track.

## 11. Command Deck

When you must draw and the Command deck is empty, shuffle its discard pile.
Then lose 1 Obligation or 1 Wealth. Repeated command consumes the kingdom.

## 12. Reform and Legacy

Reform is earned from Burhs, roads, alliances, law, and administration on
Command cards. It has no universal passive bonus. Scenario objectives and
cards state how it matters.

At Scenario end, judge the kingdom:

- **0-9, Survival:** Wessex remains, but another invasion may undo it.
- **10-14, Recovery:** Alfred leaves a defensible kingdom with hard debts.
- **15-18, Enduring Realm:** military obligation and royal authority reinforce
  one another.
- **19+, The Great:** survival became durable government, not merely memory.

No title is awarded if more regions are Exhausted than Ready.
