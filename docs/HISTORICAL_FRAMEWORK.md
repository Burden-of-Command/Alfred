# Historical Framework

## Period

The full design horizon is Alfred's reign, 871-899, with focused scenarios
covering shorter episodes. The game should distinguish three broad phases:

1. **Crisis, 871-878:** accession during war, repeated campaigning, collapse,
   refuge, recovery, and Edington.
2. **Reconstruction, c. 879-892:** settlement, fortified defense, military and
   administrative reform, London, and Mercian cooperation.
3. **Renewed invasion, 892-896:** multiple mobile Viking forces, fortified
   camps, pursuit, divided response, and endurance.

Dates and sequences may be compressed for play, but compression must be
identified in the notes.

## Historical Themes

### Military Obligation

The fyrd was not a permanently deployed field army. The design uses regional
readiness to represent the burdens of service, travel, provisioning, harvest,
garrison duty, and repeated campaigning without claiming a precise universal
system for every place and year.

### Burhs and Infrastructure

Fortified places, maintained routes, crossings, and regional obligations form
a connected defense. The later surviving *Burghal Hidage* is valuable evidence
for this system, but its precise dating and relationship to Alfred's measures
remain debated. The game should not present every listed burh or assessment as
an uncontested plan personally executed by Alfred.

### Mobile Viking Warfare

Viking forces should be capable of arriving by sea, using rivers, establishing
fortified bases, dispersing, joining, raiding, bargaining, and leaving the
theater. They are armies with changing strategic purposes, not a uniform
faceless pressure track.

### Negotiated Rule

The war includes tribute, oaths, hostages, baptism, settlement, alliance, and
boundary-making. Negotiation can be prudent without being reliable or free.

### Kingship and Reform

Military survival supports, and is supported by, wider royal authority:
lawgiving, administration, learning, religious patronage, coinage, fortified
settlement, and dynastic preparation. These should appear as costly strategic
commitments rather than a catalog of achievements.

## Historical Personalities

The design may include:

- Alfred;
- Æthelred, for the 871 prelude;
- Guthrum;
- Hastein;
- Edward, Alfred's son;
- Æthelred and Æthelflæd of Mercia;
- Ealhswith;
- leading ealdormen and bishops;
- unnamed regional leaders where the record does not support certainty.

Named figures should appear only when they create a historical decision or
strategic relationship. The game should not become a character collection.

## Treatment of Uncertainty

The surviving sources were written for particular purposes and do not provide
a modern operational record. The project will:

- distinguish attested events from design inference;
- avoid false precision about troop numbers and routes;
- identify disputed locations and dates;
- avoid presenting later institutions as fully formed at Alfred's accession;
- use historical notes to explain deliberate compression;
- prefer plausible strategic relationships over invented detail.

## Preliminary Reference Points

Research should begin with:

- the *Anglo-Saxon Chronicle*;
- Asser's *Life of King Alfred*;
- the *Burghal Hidage* and scholarship on its dating and interpretation;
- the treaty associated with Alfred and Guthrum;
- archaeological and numismatic work on burhs, London, and regional power;
- modern military and political studies of ninth-century Wessex and Mercia.

This file is a framework, not a completed historical bibliography.
