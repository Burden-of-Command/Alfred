# Version 0.6 Evaluation

## Current Standing

Version 0.6 is an advanced print-and-play prototype, not a publicly proven
9-rated game. On the evidence currently available:

- **present prototype estimate:** 8.1/10;
- **design potential after successful blind testing:** 8.7-9.0/10;
- **confidence in either estimate:** moderate-low until human data exists.

The central mechanism is already exceptional: every field force can be traced
to land whose readiness, harvest, and future defense pay for that service.
Royal Priorities now vary the player's reserve power and long-term ambition
without changing the round sequence.

## Improvements Since 0.5

- Eight Royal Priorities create different openings and ambitions.
- Priority effects are one-use and face up; they add no phase or track.
- S02 has a fairer western recovery path while remaining severe.
- The simulator now models Council and Orders as simultaneous capacities.
- Simulation output distinguishes structured core objectives from textual
  Historical Mandates.
- Card generation clears stale files and checks a 48-card product.

## Remaining Evidence Needed

The design should not be called a 9+ game until blind tests demonstrate:

- median third-play rating of at least 8.5;
- at least 70% immediate replay interest;
- rules questions repeated by fewer than 15% of testers;
- scenario win rates within their intended bands;
- no dominant opening or Royal Priority;
- median opposition resolution below 20 seconds; and
- median play time below 65 minutes.

The next gains should come from observed play, wording fixes, and scenario
tuning. Adding another subsystem now would more likely lower the rating.
