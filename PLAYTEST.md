# Playtest Protocol

## Record Each Game

1. Scenario, result, score, duration, and strategy.
2. Royal Priority choices offered, the one selected, when Resolve was used,
   and whether its Legacy condition changed play.
3. Final tracks, regional Readiness, active Burhs, fortified camps, and Hosts.
4. Every Muster: gathering region, contributing regions, and why those regions
   were selected.
5. Every Service-3 choice: Release or Retain, and what the choice enabled.
6. Every Harvest choice: fyrds released, homes depleted, and Wealth gained.
7. Steward orders and which region was chosen over military tempo.
8. Command cards used for Command versus Kingdom.
9. Battles by purpose: Delay, Drive Off, Force Decision, and Blockade.
10. Negotiations, Oaths, broken terms, and settlements.
11. The hardest geographic decision and any turn with an obvious answer.
12. Rules questions and opposition-resolution time.

## Difficulty Targets

These are targets for players who have completed one learning game:

| Difficulty | Scenarios | Target win rate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1, learning | S01 | 60-75% |
| 2, standard | S03-S05 | 45-60% |
| 3, severe | S02, S07 | 30-45% |
| 4, ordeal | S06, S08 | 20-35% |

Survival without completing the Mandate is not a win.

## System Targets

- A Muster decision changes because of regional Readiness in 80% of games.
- Release and Retain are each chosen at least 25% of Service-3 decisions.
- At least one fyrd is retained through Harvest in 25-60% of Harvest rounds.
- Steward is used in 15-40% of rounds, without becoming an automatic repair.
- No region contributes to more than 55% of all Musters across repeat plays.
- Danish Design resolves in under 20 seconds.
- Players can name each Host's target before moving it.
- Every battle purpose is used in at least 10% of relevant confrontations.
- At least one fortified camp appears in 60% of S04-S08 games.
- Command/Kingdom card use remains between 35/65 and 65/35.
- No scenario has one opening used by more than 70% of experienced players.
- No Royal Priority is selected from a two-card offer more than 75% of the
  time, and each Priority changes at least one recorded decision.
- Median play time remains below 65 minutes.

## Questions

Rate 1-10:

- The map, not the dice, created my hardest decision.
- I understood which region paid for each army I raised.
- The Service clock made returning the fyrd tempting before it became mandatory.
- Burhs changed routes and timing rather than merely adding bonuses.
- Danish purposes were predictable without becoming harmless.
- Negotiation was strategically credible but politically costly.
- The scenario demanded a strategy different from the previous one.
- I understood why I won or lost.
- The ending judged the condition of Wessex, not only battle results.
- I wanted to try another scenario immediately.

Simulator results are alarms, not evidence of quality. Human play determines
whether the decisions are legible, tense, and historically meaningful.
